Lecture Scope — Both Transcripts

What Was Actually Emphasized

The first lecture introduced FA synthesis as the last major metabolic pathway of the year, framed around the fed-state storage program. The second lecture (Biochemistry of Diabetes) revisited the same pathway in the context of obesity and T2DM, adding fructose metabolism, selective insulin resistance, AMPK, and the downstream complications of chronic hyperglycemia.

  • Fatty acid synthesis: cytoplasm, liver-heavy, driven by high insulin/glucose.
  • Palmitate is the direct mammalian product; essential fatty acids must come from the diet.
  • In T2DM, fat storage pathways show selective insulin resistance — ACC remains responsive even when muscle/adipose GLUT4 does not.
  • Fructose bypasses PFK regulation and is almost entirely processed by the liver into fat.
  • AMPK is the central energy sensor connecting metformin, exercise, and metabolic disease.
I · Orientation

High-Level Logic of the Pathway

Beta-oxidation removes two carbons per cycle. Fatty acid synthesis does the mirror image: it adds two carbons per cycle until the chain reaches C16 palmitate.

Product to Remember

Palmitate is the direct product of the synthase pathway in mammals. It can later be elongated or desaturated, but essential omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids still have to come from the diet.

Tissue Emphasis

Dr. Spicer explicitly said that this pathway is done mostly in the liver. Adipose tissue can do some synthesis but not nearly to the same degree.

II · Carbon Export

Why Citrate Has to Carry the Carbon Out

Excess glucose goes through glycolysis to pyruvate. Pyruvate enters mitochondria and becomes acetyl CoA, but acetyl CoA cannot simply cross into the cytoplasm, so the carbon leaves the mitochondria as citrate.

1

High ATP and high NADH slow the lower TCA steps

In the fed state, citrate builds up because the cell already has plenty of energy.

2

Acetyl CoA combines with oxaloacetate to make citrate

Citrate becomes the exportable form of the acetyl units that will later be used for synthesis.

3

ATP citrate lyase regenerates cytosolic acetyl CoA

Once citrate is in the cytoplasm, it is cleaved back into acetyl CoA plus oxaloacetate.

4

Oxaloacetate recycles and helps generate NADPH

Oxaloacetate can move through malate to pyruvate, and malic enzyme produces NADPH during that return loop.

Anaplerotic Point

Pyruvate carboxylase replenishes oxaloacetate so the TCA cycle does not get drained while citrate keeps leaving.

NADPH Sources Mentioned

Dr. Spicer specifically named malic enzyme and glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase as the major sources of NADPH for this pathway.

III · Regulation

ACC Commits the Cell to Fatty Acid Synthesis

Acetyl CoA carboxylase (ACC) converts a 2-carbon acetyl CoA into a 3-carbon malonyl CoA. This was the main regulated step of the lecture.

Reaction

Acetyl CoA → Malonyl CoA

ACC adds a carbon first, then that extra carbon is lost later during elongation. The ATP spent here helps power chain extension.

Activation

Citrate and Insulin

Citrate promotes polymerization of inactive ACC protomers into the active form. Insulin activates a phosphatase that removes the inhibitory phosphate.

Inhibition

Palmitoyl CoA, AMP, Glucagon, Epinephrine

Palmitoyl CoA gives product inhibition, and low-energy or fasting/stress signals push ACC toward the inactive phosphorylated state through AMPK-related signaling.

Metabolic Switch

Once ACC makes malonyl CoA, that malonyl CoA blocks CPT-I. This keeps newly synthesized long-chain fatty acids from immediately re-entering mitochondria for beta-oxidation.

Insulin-Induced Enzymes Named in the Transcript

Insulin was said to increase expression of ATP citrate lyase, malic enzyme, glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase, and ACC.

IV · Chain Building

Fatty Acid Synthase Repeats the Same Basic Cycle

The transcript did not linger on every domain name. The main idea was the repeated addition of two carbons from malonyl CoA until palmitate is released.

1

Malonyl CoA brings in the next two carbons

The carbon that ACC added is lost, leaving a net two-carbon extension of the growing chain.

2

Reduction steps use NADPH

This is why fed-state NADPH generation matters so much for synthesis.

3

The cycle repeats until the chain reaches 16 carbons

The final product that leaves the synthase complex is palmitate.

Numbers to Remember

Each two-carbon addition costs 1 ATP at the ACC step and uses 2 NADPH during the reduction steps.

V · Storage Form

TAG Synthesis Depends on the Glycerol Backbone

Triacylglycerol formation was presented as adding fatty acids onto glycerol-3-phosphate. The key tissue difference was how liver and adipose get that glycerol-3-phosphate.

Liver

Two ways to make the backbone

Liver can generate glycerol-3-phosphate from glycolytic DHAP and also has glycerol kinase, so it can phosphorylate free glycerol directly.

Adipose

Needs glucose uptake

Adipose lacks glycerol kinase, so it depends on glycolysis to make glycerol-3-phosphate from glucose-derived DHAP.

Circulation

VLDL and LPL finish the story

Liver packages TAG into VLDL. Lipoprotein lipase releases fatty acids for peripheral uptake, storage, or oxidation.

Why Adipose Needs the Fed State

Because adipose cannot use free glycerol directly, it needs glucose uptake in order to keep making the glycerol-3-phosphate backbone required for TAG storage.

VII · Type 2 Diabetes

Insulin Resistance vs. Insulin Deficiency — and Why It Takes 10 Years

Type 1 diabetes = decreased insulin production (beta-cell failure, abrupt). Type 2 diabetes = decreased insulin response (peripheral insensitivity, 10-year gradual process). Plenty of insulin is present early in T2DM.

1

Insulin resistance begins in peripheral tissues

Driven largely by visceral adipose tissue macrophages (especially M2, pro-inflammatory) that pump out inflammatory cytokines and increase lipolysis → more free fatty acids → these interfere with the insulin signaling cascade.

2

Pancreas compensates by making more insulin

Hyperinsulinemia develops. Because insulin secretion depends on glucose metabolism → ATP → Ca²⁺ entry, the overworked pancreatic beta cells generate excess ROS from the ETC, causing progressive oxidative damage.

3

Beta-cell failure tips the patient into frank diabetes

When insulin levels finally crash, blood glucose takes off and the diagnosis is made — often 10 years after the process started.

Selective Insulin Resistance — The Key Weirdness

In T2DM the insulin-insensitive pathways (muscle/adipose GLUT4 uptake, gluconeogenesis suppression, vasodilation) stop responding, but the fatty acid synthesis pathway — including ACC — keeps responding to the elevated insulin. Why? No one knows. The result: you keep making fat even as your blood glucose is rising.

Diagnostic Numbers (from Lecture)

Fasting glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL = diabetes (pre-diabetes: 100–125). HbA1c ≥ 6.5% = diabetes (pre-diabetes: 5.7–6.4%). Two fasting readings are required to confirm — one off result is not enough. HbA1c reflects the non-enzymatic glycation (Maillard reaction) that accumulates on hemoglobin over ~3 months of erythrocyte lifespan.

Glucose Tolerance Test vs. Fasting Glucose

A glucose tolerance test measures insulin sensitivity of muscle and fat (the post-prandial spike and its clearance via GLUT4). A fasting glucose measures how well the liver is restraining glucose output. They test different parts of the same story.

VIII · Fructose Metabolism

Why Fructose Makes More Visceral Fat Than Glucose

High-fructose corn syrup was introduced in the 1970s–80s and added to almost everything. Fructose is metabolized almost entirely by the liver via an unregulated pathway, which differs fundamentally from how glucose is handled.

Step 1 — Fructokinase

The unregulated entry enzyme

Fructokinase phosphorylates fructose to fructose-1-phosphate. Unlike hexokinase/glucokinase, it has no product feedback: the phosphate cannot be removed, so fructose-1-phosphate accumulates. Clinically this can trigger gout by generating excess uric acid as a byproduct.

Step 2 — Bypass of PFK

Enters glycolysis below the key control point

Fructose-1-phosphate is cleaved (by aldolase B) and the fragments enter glycolysis below phosphofructokinase — the main regulated step. There is no speed limit. The carbon pours into the liver unimpeded.

Fate in the Liver

Mostly fatty acids + triglycerides

The liver converts the bulk of dietary fructose to acetyl CoA and then to fatty acids + VLDL. Compared with glucose, fructose produces much more visceral fat (not just subcutaneous fat). Studies show identical subcutaneous fat gain between glucose and fructose but dramatically higher visceral fat with fructose.

Ethanol Parallel

Dr. Spicer explicitly called out the fructose–ethanol parallel: both are processed almost entirely by the liver, both overwhelm the system and drive fatty acid synthesis, both preferentially cause hepatic/visceral fat and NAFLD (now MASLD).

Glycogen Bonus — Pre-Exercise

In normal/active amounts, fructose-1-phosphate mimics glucose-1-phosphate and inhibits glycogen phosphorylase, promoting glycogen storage. Pre-exercise fructose loading can be beneficial for this reason.

Glucokinase Release

Fructose-1-phosphate can release glucokinase from its nuclear inhibitor protein, just like fructose-6-phosphate does. This means eating fructose actually accelerates hepatic glucose metabolism simultaneously.

Hereditary Fructose Intolerance

Mutations in aldolase B (the enzyme that cleaves fructose-1-phosphate) cause fructose-1-phosphate to pile up, which is toxic. Even without the mutation, this enzyme is slow enough that fructose-1-phosphate accumulates with a high-fructose diet.

IX · AMPK

AMP-Activated Protein Kinase — the Energy Sensor

AMPK is activated by high AMP (low energy state). Once active, it shuts off every pathway that uses energy (all the synthetic/anabolic ones on the left of the metabolic map) and turns on everything that makes energy.

AMPK Turns OFF

Anabolic / energy-consuming

Fatty acid synthesis (phosphorylates and inactivates ACC), cholesterol synthesis, protein synthesis, gluconeogenesis.

AMPK Turns ON

Catabolic / energy-generating

Glycolysis (glucose uptake), fatty acid oxidation (by reducing malonyl CoA → CPT-I is freed), mitochondrial biogenesis (more efficient and more mitochondria).

What Activates AMPK

AMP, exercise, metformin

High AMP (low ATP state) is the allosteric activator. Exercise depletes ATP → raises AMP → activates AMPK. Metformin is one drug that targets this pathway, inhibiting ACC and suppressing fatty acid synthesis.

ACC is the Key AMPK Substrate Here

When AMPK phosphorylates ACC, ACC is inactivated. Malonyl CoA drops. CPT-I is freed. Long-chain fatty acids can enter mitochondria. Beta-oxidation resumes. This is why both exercise and metformin improve fat metabolism through AMPK → ACC inhibition.

Exercise as a "Wonder Drug"

Dr. Spicer called exercise a wonder drug: it uses ATP → raises AMP → activates AMPK → triggers all the downstream metabolic improvements (glycolysis, FA oxidation, mitochondrial biogenesis, reduced FA synthesis). Muscle growth also raises basal metabolic rate because muscle has far more mitochondria than white adipose tissue.

FAS Inhibitors as Chemo — Why They Fail

Cancer cells upregulate FAS for anabolic growth. FAS inhibitors were explored as chemotherapy, but they turned out to be very cytotoxic. Why? Blocking FAS builds up malonyl CoA upstream → malonyl CoA blocks CPT-I → fatty acid oxidation is wiped out → cellular energy production crashes. ACC is a much safer target than FAS itself because blocking ACC does not build up toxic intermediates.

X · Hyperglycemia Complications

Four Pathways That Turn Chronic High Glucose into Organ Damage

Tissues damaged in diabetes are those whose glucose uptake is not regulated by insulin (neurons, renal tubules, retinal vessels, lens) — they cannot control intracellular glucose concentration and suffer the consequences.

PathwayMechanismClinical Consequence
AGE Formation Non-enzymatic glycation (Maillard reaction) — glucose attaches to proteins. HbA1c is just a measurable marker; the same process happens on collagen, basement membranes, etc. Nephropathy, retinopathy, neuropathy, accelerated atherosclerosis
Polyol Pathway Excess glucose is reduced to sorbitol (same enzyme that makes galactitol in galactosemia). Sorbitol accumulates in cells that don't easily export it. Cataracts (lens), peripheral neuropathy, retinopathy
PKC Activation Excess lipolysis (from insulin resistance) produces diacylglycerol (DAG) as a breakdown intermediate → DAG activates PKC → PKC interferes with insulin signaling. Worsens insulin resistance; vascular dysfunction
Hexosamine Pathway Glucose flux into hexosamine synthesis increases → promotes pro-inflammatory gene expression. Systemic inflammation; further insulin resistance
VLDL & Triglycerides in T2DM

In T2DM: the liver is making more fatty acids (ACC still insulin-sensitive) AND LPL in adipose is insulin-resistant so VLDL is cleared more slowly. Result: triglycerides are the most elevated lipid in early T2DM. Fasting triglycerides on a lab panel primarily reflect VLDL levels (chylomicrons are gone after an overnight fast).

Neonatal Hypoglycemia — Clinical Vignette

A newborn from a poorly controlled diabetic mother was hyperinsulinemic in utero (the fetal pancreas was responding to chronically elevated maternal glucose). After delivery, maternal glucose is gone, but neonatal insulin levels remain high. With minimal glycogen stores, blood glucose plummets → neonatal hypoglycemia and seizures.

XI · Adipose Biology

Brown vs. White vs. Beige Adipose Tissue

The color of adipose tissue reflects its mitochondrial density. Brown fat is warm and thermogenic; white fat is storage-only with almost no mitochondria. Exercise can convert white into beige — an intermediate with thermogenic properties.

Brown Adipose

Dense mitochondria, thermogenesis

Uses uncoupled ETC (UCP1) to generate heat instead of ATP. Abundant in neonates (explains why babies are warm). Adults have less, mostly around the neck/supraclavicular region.

White Adipose

Storage, few mitochondria

Subcutaneous and visceral depots. Visceral white fat in obesity is infiltrated by M2 macrophages that secrete pro-inflammatory cytokines → the root driver of peripheral insulin resistance.

Beige Adipose

Exercise-induced browning

Exercise signals white adipose to make more mitochondria → "beige" tissue with partial thermogenic function. Also increases basal metabolic rate. Switching 10% of body composition from fat to muscle raises BMR substantially because of the mitochondrial difference.

BMR and Mitochondria

Basal metabolic rate is fundamentally a measure of mitochondrial activity. Muscle tissue has far more mitochondria per gram than white adipose. Gaining muscle mass raises BMR even at rest — this is the metabolic rationale for resistance training in obesity/T2DM management.
