38A: Basic Organization of the Head & Neck

The Skull (22 bones total)

The skull is divided into 2 main parts by a bony plate called the basicranium.

Neurocranium (8 bones)

A hollow vault containing the brain, made of 5 types of fused bones:

  • Frontal (1), Parietal (2), Occipital (1), Sphenoid (2), Temporal (2)

Viscerocranium (14 bones)

Bony arches surrounding oral and nasal passageways, separated by dense connective tissue periosteum:

  • Nasal (2), Lacrimal (2), Palatine (2), Zygomatic (2)
  • Maxilla (2), Inferior nasal concha (2), Mandible (1), Vomer (1)

Neurocranium, Viscerocranium & 14 Facial Bones

Cervical Spine (C1–C7)

VertebraKey Features
Atlas (C1)Lacks body & spinous process; ring-like structure articulating with occipital condyles
Axis (C2)Prominent odontoid process (dens) — bony projection for head rotation; articulates with atlas
C3–C6Small vertebral bodies, bifid spinous processes, transverse foramina (for vertebral arteries)
C7 (Vertebra Prominens)Long, non-bifid spinous process; easily palpable at base of neck
Development: Arises from somites (sclerotomes) of paraxial mesoderm. Notochord contributes to disks. The dens is part of C2 but embryologically comes from C1.

38B: Cervical Spine, Hyoid & Basicranium

C1 (Atlas), C2 (Axis) & C3 vertebrae in detail

Cervical Vertebrae Detail

VertebraKey Features
C1 (Atlas)2 lateral masses, 2 arches, 2 transverse processes. Each TP splits into anterior costal & posterior transverse. Body fused to C2. Articulates with occipital condyles.
C2 (Axis)Body with 2 lateral masses. Dens (odontoid process) extends upward into anterior arch of C1. Dens derived from C1 during development — pivot for C1 rotation. 2 laminae → bifid spinous process.
C3Lateral masses with articular surfaces separated from body by pedicle. Laminae extend posteromedially. Zygapophyseal joints between adjacent vertebrae.
C3–C7 GeneralSmall vertebral bodies, bifid spinous processes (except C7), transverse foramina, uncinate processes.

Hyoid Bone

Hyoid Bone — Anterior & Lateral Views

U-shaped bone suspended in the anterior neck. Does NOT articulate with any other bone.

  • Function: Attachment for anterior neck muscles; keeps airway open
  • Position: Level of C3–C4, in the angle between mandible and thyroid cartilage
  • Parts: Body (central), Greater horns (cornua), Lesser horns (cornua)
  • Development: From 2nd (Reichert's cartilage) & 3rd pharyngeal arches
    • Lesser horns & upper body → Second arch
    • Greater horns & lower body → Third arch

Basicranium

A platform separating the neurocranium from the viscerocranium.

Composition: Basiocciput + Basisphenoid + Temporal (petrous) + Ethmoid bone.

Basicranium — Sphenoid, Occipital & Temporal Bones

BoneKey Features
SphenoidThickened midline → basisphenoid. SOF transmits CN III, IV, VI, V₁. Foramen rotundum → V₂. 2 Pterygoid plates from greater wing. Origin: neural crest cells
OccipitalThickened midline → basioccipital. Divided by foramen magnum: squamous (sup.), lateral, basilar (inf.). Superior nuchal line divides squamous into planum occipitale & planum nuchale. Origin: paraxial mesoderm (somites)
TemporalThickened petrous portion. Wedge between sphenoid & occipital. Squamous portion, zygomatic processes, styloid process. Petrous: cochlea (hearing), labyrinthine organ (balance). Origin: both neural crest & mesoderm

38C: Cervical Fascial Layers

Cervical fascia is fibrous connective tissue that surrounds and compartmentalizes neck structures. Divided into superficial and deep layers, arranged as concentric circular rings.

Cross-section showing cervical fascial layers & carotid sheath

LayerAlso Known AsContents
Superficial Cervical FasciaSubcutaneousCutaneous vasculature, nerves, lymphatics, platysma (CN VII)
Superficial Layer of Deep Cervical FasciaDeep InvestingTrapezius, SCM, accessory nerve (CN XI)
Middle Layer of Deep Cervical FasciaPretrachealAnt: strap (hyoid) muscles · Post: trachea & esophagus · Laterally merges with carotid sheath
Visceral Fascia & Carotid Sheath—Extends from cranial base around pharyngeal constrictors → thorax (fascia of mediastinum). Carotid sheath: common carotid a., vagus n., internal jugular v.
Deep Layer of Deep Cervical FasciaPrevertebralRectus, longus, scalene muscles; paraspinal muscles. Attaches to transverse processes.
⚠ Danger Space: Located behind the pharynx, inferiorly closed at T4, laterally between two carotid sheaths. Infections can rapidly travel along fascial planes and are very difficult to treat with antibiotics.

38D: Arterial Supply, Venous & Lymphatic Drainage

Arterial Supply

Main arteries originate from the aortic arch (left) and brachiocephalic trunk (right).

Carotid & Vertebrobasilar Systems with Circle of Willis

Carotid System

  • Common Carotid: Right = branch of brachiocephalic; Left = direct from aortic arch. Splits at C4/C5.
  • External Carotid: Supplies scalp & face. Branches: Superior thyroid, Ascending pharyngeal, Lingual, Facial, Occipital, Posterior auricular, Maxillary, Superficial temporal
  • Internal Carotid: Supplies orbit & anterior cranium. No branches in the neck. Passes anterior to transverse processes of upper 3 cervical vertebrae → enters temporal bone.

Vertebrobasilar System

  • Arises from subclavian → supplies cervical vertebral column & posterior cranium
  • At C6, enters transverse foramina → proceeds upward to C1 → foramen magnum
  • Vertebral arteries fuse → basilar artery → anastomoses with ICA in Circle of Willis

Thyrocervical Trunk

From 1st portion of subclavian, near anterior scalene. Branches: inferior thyroid, transverse scapular, superficial cervical.

Pharynx

Three Divisions of the Pharynx

  • Nasopharynx: Behind nasal cavity, above soft palate
  • Oropharynx: Behind oral cavity, soft palate to hyoid bone
  • Hypopharynx (Laryngopharynx): Hyoid bone to esophagus/larynx
