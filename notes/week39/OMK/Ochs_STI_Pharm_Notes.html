Beta-Lactams

Tetracyclines (Doxycycline, Minocycline, Tetracycline)

PropertyDetails
MOABind 30S ribosomal subunit reversibly → inhibit protein synthesis → bacteriostatic (inhibiting growth)
SpectrumAerobic Gm+ and Gm−, Rickettsia, Mycoplasma, Chlamydia, CA-MRSA (broad spectrum)
AEsTooth discoloration (avoid in children < 8 and pregnancy), photosensitivity (doxycycline, tetracycline), esophageal ulcers. Minocycline: skin pigmentation, dizziness, vertigo.
CounselingSwallow with full glass of water
DIsDairy products and cationic antacids → decreased absorption
CIChildren < 8 years old
PregnancyNO

Macrolides (Azithromycin, Erythromycin, Clarithromycin)

PropertyDetails
MOABind 50S ribosomal subunit → inhibit protein synthesis → primarily bacteriostatic
AEsGI (diarrhea), cholestatic hepatitis (rare), QT interval prolongation. Erythromycin: hepatotoxicity. Clarithromycin: altered taste.
DIsCYP3A4 inhibitors — erythromycin and clarithromycin. Azithromycin does NOT inhibit CYP3A4.
CIHypersensitivity
PregnancyAzithromycin & Erythromycin: use if clearly needed. Clarithromycin: risk–benefit.

Aminoglycosides (Gentamicin)

PropertyDetails
MOABind 30S ribosomal subunit irreversibly → inhibit protein synthesis → bactericidal (killing bacteria)
AEsNephrotoxicity, ototoxicity, neuromuscular blockade risk
CIHypersensitivity
PregnancyOnly if absolutely necessary

Fluoroquinolones (Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin)

PropertyDetails
MOAInhibit 2 bacterial DNA topoisomerases: DNA gyrase (type II) → target for Gm−; Topoisomerase IV → target for Gm+. Concentration-dependent killing, long post-antibiotic effect (PAE).
AEsTendon rupture, QT prolongation, glucose excursions (hypo/hyperglycemia), phototoxicity, peripheral neuropathy, CNS effects (dizziness)
DIsCaffeine (CYP1A2 inhibition) and cations (dairy, antacids)
CIPregnancy & pediatrics — cartilage damage
PregnancyRisk–benefit should be evaluated

Nitroimidazoles (Metronidazole, Tinidazole)

PropertyDetails
MOAProdrug → nitro group reduced under anaerobic conditions → forms reactive radical → disrupts DNA/nucleic acid synthesis. Only works in anaerobic bacteria and protozoa.
PKWell absorbed, widely distributed, extensively metabolized, renally excreted
SpectrumB. fragilis, C. difficile, H. pylori, E. histolytica, Giardia, Trichomonas vaginalis, parasitic infections
AEsAnorexia, nausea, vomiting, metallic taste; take with food. Disulfiram-like reaction with ethanol (nausea, vomiting, sweating, flushing, palpitations, dyspnea) BOARDS
CIHypersensitivity, alcohol use
PregnancyMetronidazole: risk–benefit. Tinidazole: NO.
Disulfiram-like reaction update: Studies have failed to demonstrate that nitroimidazoles increase acetaldehyde in blood or that signs of a disulfiram reaction actually occur. CDC no longer recommends refraining from ethanol while taking metronidazole. BUT it remains a favorite BOARD question and still comes up as a significant drug interaction.

Lincosamides (Clindamycin)

PropertyDetails
MOABinds 50S ribosomal subunit
AEsGI upset, diarrhea, risk of C. difficile colitis
CIHypersensitivity
PregnancyUse if clearly needed

Nucleoside Analogues (Acyclovir, Valacyclovir, Famciclovir)

PropertyDetails
MOAConverted to active nucleoside triphosphates by viral and host cell kinases. Active forms compete with endogenous nucleoside triphosphates and competitively inhibit viral DNA polymerase. Only acyclovir is incorporated into viral DNA and causes chain termination (lacks 3'-OH). Ganciclovir and penciclovir do NOT cause chain termination.
ProdrugsValacyclovir → acyclovir. Famciclovir → penciclovir.
ActionPalliative, NOT curative. Shorten symptoms, decrease frequency of recurrences and viral shedding with long-term suppression.
AEsGI upset, headache, crystalluria and nephrotoxicity (IV acyclovir), phlebitis (IV, 15%)
CIHypersensitivity
PregnancyUse if clearly needed — all three likely safe. Suppression of recurrent genital herpes near term may reduce need for C-section to avoid neonatal herpes.

LO 16 — Select Drug Regimens for STIs (Clinical Cases)

Key approach: Be able to tell what STI they have, what drug to give, and reasons to use/not use a drug (AE, pregnancy, allergy, etc.)

Gonorrhea (Neisseria gonorrhoeae — Gram− diplococcus)

Transmission during delivery → ophthalmia neonatorum

ScenarioRegimenNotes
Uncomplicated (cervicitis, urethritis, rectal) Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM single dose (<150 kg; 1 g if ≥150 kg) If chlamydia not excluded → add doxycycline 100 mg PO BID x 7 days
Disseminated (arthritis-dermatitis) Ceftriaxone 1 g IM or IV q24h Same chlamydia coverage rule
Meningitis / Endocarditis Ceftriaxone 1–2 g IV q24h Same chlamydia coverage rule
Conjunctivitis (adults) Ceftriaxone 1 g IM single dose + saline lavage
Ophthalmia neonatorum prevention Erythromycin 0.5% ophthalmic ointment each eye at birth
Ophthalmia neonatorum treatment Ceftriaxone 25–50 mg/kg IV or IM (max 250 mg)

Alternate Regimens

SituationRegimen
Ceftriaxone not availableCefixime 800 mg PO single dose
Cephalosporin allergyGentamicin 240 mg IM + Azithromycin 2 g PO single dose
PregnancySame as non-pregnant EXCEPT use azithromycin instead of doxycycline for chlamydia coverage
Gonorrhea pearls: Ceftriaxone in ALL cases (dose varies). ~5% cross-sensitivity with PCN (highest 1st-gen, rare <1% with 3rd-gen). Instruct patients to refer sex partners for evaluation.

Chlamydia (Chlamydia trachomatis)

Neonatal conjunctivitis in up to 50%. Pneumonia in up to 16%.

ScenarioRegimen
Adults/Adolescents (non-pregnant)Doxycycline 100 mg PO BID x 7 days (DOC)
Alternative (questionable compliance)Azithromycin 1 g PO single dose
AlternativeLevofloxacin 500 mg PO daily x 7 days
PregnancyAzithromycin 1 g PO single dose (alt: Amoxicillin 500 mg TID x 7 days)
Neonatal conjunctivitis/pneumoniaErythromycin (DOC). Azithromycin = alternative in infants.
Chlamydia pearls: Abstain from sex until 7 days after Tx complete. Refer sex partners if intercourse within 60 days.

Syphilis (Treponema pallidum)

StagePrimary TherapyPCN-Allergic Alternative
Primary / Secondary / Early latent (<1 yr) Benzathine penicillin G IM single dose Tetracycline or Doxycycline PO x 14 days OR Ceftriaxone IM/IV daily x 8–10 days
Late latent / Unknown / Tertiary Benzathine penicillin G IM weekly x 3 Tetracycline or Doxycycline PO x 4 weeks
Neurosyphilis Aqueous crystalline penicillin G IV q4h or continuous x 10–14 days Procaine penicillin IM daily + probenecid x 10–14 days (if compliance assured)
Pregnancy Penicillin is the ONLY option — NO alternative. Skin test and desensitize if allergic.
Congenital T. pallidum crosses placenta at any time. Penicillin at dose appropriate for stage. No proven alternatives for children.
Probenecid in neurosyphilis: inhibits renal tubular penicillin secretion → keeps penicillin levels higher for longer.
Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction: Tell patients about fever, headache, & myalgia within 24 hours of treatment. Test all syphilis patients for HIV.

Bacterial Vaginosis (BV)

Organisms: B. fragilis, Prevotella sp, Mobiluncus sp, G. vaginalis, M. hominis. Malodorous vaginal discharge with fishy smell. Treat all symptomatic women. BV increases risk of adverse pregnancy outcomes (premature labor, delivery complications). Follow-up not needed if symptoms resolve.

RecommendedAlternatives
Metronidazole 500 mg PO BID x 7 days
or Metronidazole gel 0.75% intravag daily x 5 days
or Clindamycin cream 2% intravag at bedtime x 7 days 		Clindamycin 300 mg PO BID x 7 days
or Clindamycin ovules intravag x 3 days
or Secnidazole 2 g granules single dose
or Tinidazole 2 g PO daily x 2 days or 1 g daily x 5 days
Pregnancy: Same regimen as non-pregnant.

Trichomoniasis (Trichomonas vaginalis — protozoan)

Males: often asymptomatic. Females: malodorous yellow-green discharge, vaginal irritation, cervical petechiae.

Treatment
Metronidazole 500 mg PO BID x 7 days (including pregnant women)
Alternative: Tinidazole 2 g PO single dose

If treatment failure → increase dose or duration. Treat sexual partners simultaneously.

HPV (Human Papillomavirus)

Most common viral STI in U.S. Types 6 & 11 → genital warts. Types 16 & 18 → cervical cancer.

LocationOptions
Vaginal / Cervical / Anal warts Cryotherapy OR TCA/BCA 80–90% (repeat weekly) OR Surgical removal
External anogenital warts Patient-applied: Podofilox†, Imiquimod†, Sinecatechins†
Provider-administered: Cryotherapy, Podophyllin†, TCA/BCA, Surgical removal
† Not in pregnancy

Pediculosis Pubis (Pthirus pubis)

RecommendedAlternative
Permethrin 1% cream rinse to affected areas, wash off after 10 min
or Pyrethrins + PBO applied, wash off after 10 min		 Malathion 0.5% lotion applied, washed off 8–12 hrs
or Ivermectin 250 μg/kg PO, repeat in 7–14 days

Pregnancy: Permethrin or pyrethrins + PBO recommended. Ivermectin = "human data suggest low risk." Malathion NOT recommended.

Machine wash/dry or dry clean clothing & bedding, or remove from body contact ≥72 hrs. Treat sexual partners within previous month.

Granuloma Inguinale (Klebsiella granulomatis) — Donovanosis

Gram− bacterium. Painless ulcerative lesions with beefy red appearance. Dx: Donovan bodies on biopsy.

RecommendedAlternatives
Azithromycin 1 g PO weekly or 500 mg daily for >3 weeks until healed Doxycycline 100 mg BID x ≥3 weeks, or Erythromycin* 500 mg QID x ≥3 weeks, or TMP-SMX DS BID x ≥3 weeks

*Pregnancy: treated with a macrolide regimen. Examine sex partners within 60 days.

Chancroid (Haemophilus ducreyi — Gram− coccobacilli)

Risk factor for HIV transmission/acquisition. Painful genital ulcers + regional lymphadenopathy. Negative for T. pallidum.

Options (any one)
Azithromycin 1 g PO single dose
Ceftriaxone 250 mg IM single dose
Ciprofloxacin 500 mg PO BID x 3 days
Erythromycin base 500 mg PO TID x 7 days

Test for HIV. Uncircumcised patients and those with HIV → more likely treatment failure. Reexamine 3–7 days. Treat sex partners within 10 days.
