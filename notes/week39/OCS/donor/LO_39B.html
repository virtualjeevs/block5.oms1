The Six Pharyngeal Arches

Development of each arch and its cranial nerve, muscles, skeletal elements, and other derivatives

📋 Overview

During weeks 4–5, neural crest cells migrate into the head and neck region and pile up between the surface ectoderm (outside) and the pharyngeal endoderm (inside). This creates paired swellings called pharyngeal arches (also called branchial arches).

Each arch contains four core components:

🦴 Skeletal / Cartilage

Bones, cartilages, and ligaments

💪 Muscles

Striated muscles that migrate with their nerve

🧠 Cranial Nerve

Motor, sensory, and/or autonomic innervation

❤️ Artery

An aortic arch artery
Key concept: As muscles of each arch migrate, they always drag their cranial nerve with them. Know the arch → know the nerve.

When Do They Appear?

Day 23: 1st pharyngeal arch forms (most cranial position)
Day 24: 2nd arch appears → 1st groove forms between them
Day 26: 3rd arch develops → 2nd groove appears
Day 28: 4th arch (last visible externally). 5th and 6th develop internally — 5th almost entirely disappears
Important: The 2nd arch overgrows the 3rd and 4th, pulling them inward → smooth neck contour. This buries a temporary cervical sinus.

1 First Pharyngeal Arch

1st Arch — The Mandibular Arch

Nerve: CN V — Trigeminal  |  Cartilage: Meckel's

Two parts: maxillary prominence (upper jaw) and mandibular prominence (lower jaw). Meckel's cartilage disappears — the mandible forms lateral to it via intramembranous ossification.

🦴 Skeletal

  • Meckel's cartilage → disappears; perichondrium → sphenomandibular ligament + anterior lig. of malleus
  • Malleus & incus (middle ear ossicles)
  • Mandible (intramembranous ossification)
  • Maxilla, zygomatic bone, part of temporal bone

💪 Muscles

  • Muscles of mastication — temporalis, masseter, med. & lat. pterygoids
  • Mylohyoid (floor of mouth)
  • Anterior belly of digastric
  • Tensor veli palatini
  • Tensor tympani

🧠 CN V (Trigeminal)

  • V2 (Maxillary) — purely sensory, upper jaw
  • V3 (Mandibular) — sensory + motor, lower jaw
  • Motor to all muscles of mastication
  • Sensory: face, mouth, nose, touch/proprioception to anterior ⅔ of tongue

❤️ Artery

Terminal maxillary artery (branch of external carotid)

2 Second Pharyngeal Arch

2nd Arch — The Hyoid Arch

Nerve: CN VII — Facial  |  Cartilage: Reichert's

Unlike Meckel's, Reichert's cartilage ossifies (endochondral ossification) to form bony structures.

🦴 Skeletal

  • Stapes (3rd ossicle) — dorsal Reichert's
  • Styloid process of temporal bone — dorsal Reichert's
  • Stylohyoid ligament — middle Reichert's
  • Lesser horn + upper body of hyoid — ventral Reichert's

💪 Muscles

  • Muscles of facial expression — frontalis, orbicularis oculi/oris, buccinator, etc.
  • Stapedius
  • Stylohyoid
  • Posterior belly of digastric
  • Platysma
  • Occipitalis, auricular muscles

🧠 CN VII (Facial)

  • Motor to all muscles of facial expression
  • Chorda tympani → crosses tympanic membrane → taste to anterior ⅔ of tongue

❤️ Arteries

  • Stapedial artery — regresses before birth
  • Hyoid artery → corticotympanic artery

3 Third Pharyngeal Arch

3rd Arch

Nerve: CN IX — Glossopharyngeal  |  Cartilage: unnamed

A smaller arch — only one muscle.

🦴 Skeletal

  • Greater horn (cornu) of hyoid
  • Lower body of hyoid

💪 Muscles

  • Stylopharyngeus — just one!

🧠 CN IX (Glossopharyngeal)

  • Motor to stylopharyngeus
  • Taste + general sensation to posterior ⅓ of tongue
  • Tympanic nerve → parasympathetics to parotid gland

❤️ Artery

Common carotid + proximal internal carotid

4 Fourth Pharyngeal Arch

4th Arch

Nerve: CN X — Vagus (Superior Laryngeal Branch)

Derivatives are mostly cartilaginous (laryngeal cartilages), not bony.

🦴 Skeletal

  • Thyroid cartilage (upper portion)
  • Corniculate & cuneiform cartilages
  • Epiglottic cartilage

💪 Muscles

  • Cricothyroid
  • Pharyngeal constrictors
  • Levator veli palatini

🧠 CN X, Superior Laryngeal

  • Motor to cricothyroid, constrictors, levator palatini
  • Sensory to root of tongue

❤️ Arteries

  • Right: Proximal subclavian
  • Left: Aortic arch
5th Pharyngeal Arch: Forms briefly then regresses. No significant adult derivatives.

6 Sixth Pharyngeal Arch

6th Arch

Nerve: CN X — Vagus (Recurrent Laryngeal Branch)

Key difference from 4th: innervated by recurrent laryngeal (not superior).

🦴 Skeletal

  • Cricoid cartilage
  • Arytenoid cartilages
  • Lower thyroid cartilage

💪 Muscles

  • All intrinsic laryngeal muscles EXCEPT cricothyroid
  • Thyroarytenoid, cricoarytenoids, transverse arytenoid, vocalis

🧠 CN X, Recurrent Laryngeal

  • Motor to intrinsic laryngeal muscles (except cricothyroid)
  • Sensory: pharynx, larynx, esophagus, tympanic membrane, external ear
  • Parasympathetic to thoracic/abdominal viscera

❤️ Arteries

  • Right: Proximal pulmonary arteries
  • Left: Ductus arteriosus

🫁 Pharyngeal Pouches

Pouches = internal (endoderm-lined) outpocketings between adjacent arches.

1st Pouch

→ Tubotympanic recess → tympanic cavity (middle ear) + Eustachian tube.

1st membrane → tympanic membrane (ectoderm outside, mesoderm middle, endoderm inside).

2nd Pouch

→ Palatine tonsil. Endoderm lines tonsillar crypts; lymphoid tissue from mesenchyme.

3rd Pouch

Splits into two — and the numbering flips:

  • Dorsal → INFERIOR parathyroid — migrates farther down!
  • Ventral → Thymus — endoderm → epithelial reticular cells + Hassall's corpuscles.

4th Pouch

  • Dorsal → SUPERIOR parathyroid (stays higher)
  • Ventral → Ultimobranchial body → enters thyroid → parafollicular C cells (calcitonin)

🧠 Parathyroid Trick

3rd pouch → Inferior (migrates far)  |  4th pouch → Superior (stays put)
"3 and 4 flip!"

〰️ Pharyngeal Clefts

Clefts = external (ectoderm-lined) grooves between adjacent arches.

1st Cleft

Only one that persists → external acoustic meatus. Auricle: anterior from 1st arch, posterior (helix + lobe) from 2nd arch.

2nd–4th Clefts

All disappear → buried as cervical sinus by 2nd arch overgrowth → normally degenerates.

Branchial Cysts & Fistulae

Branchial cyst = cervical sinus fails to degenerate → lateral neck mass anterior to SCM (often mid-adolescence).

Branchial fistula = membrane also breaks down → connects pharynx to external neck → weeps mucus.

🦻 The Ear — Putting It Together

🦻 Multi-Arch Integration

  • External ear canal → 1st cleft
  • Auricle → anterior = 1st arch, posterior = 2nd arch
  • Tympanic membrane → 1st membrane (ecto + meso + endo)
  • Middle ear + Eustachian tube → 1st pouch
  • Malleus & Incus → 1st arch  |  Stapes → 2nd arch
  • Tensor tympani → 1st arch (CN V)  |  Stapedius → 2nd arch (CN VII)
